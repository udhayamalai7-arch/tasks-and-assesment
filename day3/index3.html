At a joint press briefing at the Secretariat, Additional Chief Secretary to Government, Revenue, P. Amudha explained the sequences that led to the stampede. Home Secretary Dheeraj Kumar, DGP G. Venkatraman and ADGP (Law and Order) S. Davidson Devasirvatham also participated. Ms Amudha said the TVK announced that Mr Vijay would address the cadres by noon on Saturday whereas he came only in the night. A large number of people had gathered since morning and many became tired and dehydrated by the time Mr Vijay arrived. The venue of the event - Velusamypuram - was finalised on the request of the organisers, she said. Playing videos on large screens on how the tragedy unfolded, she said a lot of youth drove along with Mr Vijay’s vehicle to the venue which resulted in the number of people expected at the rally going up by more than a double.

The Tamil Nadu government has constituted a one-member commission of inquiry headed by former High Court Judge Justice Aruna Jagadeesan to investigate the tragic stampede incident at a political rally in Karur, which claimed 40 lives and left over 80 people injured. The incident occurred yesterday during a massive gathering organised by the Tamilaga Vetri Kazhagam (TVK) to welcome actor-turned-politician andthe party’s founder Vijay. The panel began its probe this evening with Justice Jagadeesan visiting the site where the incident took place. In a related development, Tamil Nadu Additional Director General of Police (law and order) Davidson Devasirvatham also visited the site. Later addressing the media, he clarified that the stampede occurred when the crowd surged towards actor Vijay’s vehicle and added that although there was a delay in the event, there was no lapse in security. TVK General Secretary N. Anand and four others have been booked in connection with the incident under various sections.
